What the heck is Article 34? Is it about PSD2 something? Yes it is!

PSD2 summary

Or the revised Payment Services Directive (EU) 2015/2366 is an EU Directive, administered by the European Commission (Directorate General Internal Market) to regulate payment services and payment service providers throughout the European Union (EU) and European Economic Area (EEA).

The Directive's purpose was to increase pan-European competition and participation in the payments industry also from non-banks, and to provide for a level playing field by harmonizing consumer protection and the rights and obligations for payment providers and users.

Certificate validation

Article 34 or more precisely the COMMISSION DELEGATED REGULATION (EU) 2018/389 of 27 November 2017 supplementing Directive (EU) 2015/2366 of the European Parliament and of the Council with regard to Regulatory Technical Standards (RTS) for Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) and common and secure open standards of communication.

According to the RTS on SCA and CSC (Article 34. Certificates), payment service providers shall rely on eIDAS certificates for third party providers’ (TPPs’) identification. TPPs identification shall rely on a qualified certificate, as defined in EU Regulation 910/2014 (eIDAS).

Conclusion, all ASPSPs must make sure all TPP PSD2 access requests are made by their respective certificate holder, having a valid PSD2 eIDAS certificate and authored by any of EU Qualified Certificate Providers including applicable Passporting rights.

API documentation: Just POST then GET with subscriptionId.

  • POST /certificate
  • GET /validation

Why complicate things. Onboard yourself (as TPP) or your requesting TPPs (as ASPSP) by sending a POST request with a data (body) payload using the "x509" key and paste your certificate in the value field including
"-----BEGIN CERTIFICATE-----
MDDJBDCCBuygAwIBAgIUFI. . . .
. . . wPBvdnxWtCiIFL6GLUyQU=
-----END CERTIFICATE-----"

Thats it. Really!

As simple as our POST endpoint, just put the incoming TPP certificate as a x509 key with the certificate as payload value, or even easier, the SHA-1 fingerprint in the request header together with your subscriptionId.

You will get an response in ms with real time verifications and EBA validations to rest asure that any incoming TPP request can be signed off as fully compliant.
